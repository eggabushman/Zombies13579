Helpful Material for Educational and (Mostly) Entertainment Purposes


The Walking Dead (2010-present)

Compared to the later mentioned show "Z Nation", “The Walking Dead” provides a far more surreal and realistic depiction of the challenges one must endure during the zombie apocalypse. This show especially underscores the theme that humans are our real enemies. The comic book series by the same name is just as riveting as the show and seen by many as artisticly superior. This show currently airs on AMC.

Z Nation (2014-present)

While not altogether realistic, this show provides a hilarious, entertaining, and at times cheesy depiction of what you could encounter in a zombie apocalypse. From zombie bears to cannibalistic cults, this show’s gruesome innovation is where it truly shines. This show currently airs on SyFy.

The Last of Us (2013)

"The Last of Us" is a survival horror game developed by famed studio Naughty Dog, exclusively for the PlayStation 3 and PlayStation 4 consoles. Players control Joel, a man tasked with escorting a young girl named Ellie across a post-apocalyptic United States. "The Last of Us" is not just a zombie game - it is one of the most heartfelt and gripping stories ever told. "The Last of Us" is an absolute must-play for any fan of the zombie apocalypse genre, or anyone looking for an excellent video game experience.

World War Z (2006)

Written by Max Brooks, "World War Z" is a brilliant and diverse collection of transcripts from interviews with those who survived a global zombie outbreak, or World War Z. Much can be learned from each survivor's story that will ultimately increase your likelihood of surviving the night. We do not recommend watching the recent movie of the same name. In terms of content and quality, it is nothing like this book.

The Zombie Survival Guide (2003)

Also written by Max Brooks, "The Zombie Survival Guide" is the quintessential piece of survival material that even puts our website to shame. This book is chock-full of wonderful, lesser-known survival information, and should always be kept near to anyone looking to gain the upper hand in the apocalypse.
